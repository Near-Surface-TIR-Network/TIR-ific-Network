About TIR-ific Network

Our mission, team, and ways to get involved.

Our Mission

The TIR-ific Network is building a global community dedicated to standardizing and advancing near-surface thermal infrared (TIR) imaging in ecosystems. Our mission is to create an accessible, interoperable, and scientifically rigorous network that empowers researchers to better monitor, compare, and interpret canopy temperature and ecosystem responses to climate change.

Our recent article in Eos outlines the value of TIR data, community-led standards, and the vision for a coordinated global network.

The Science of TIR

All objects above absolute zero (0 Kelvin) emit thermal radiation. TIR sensing detects this radiation to estimate surface temperature without physical contact. In ecological research, this approach provides a powerful lens into ecosystem processes by enabling non-invasive measurements of canopy temperature—a key indicator of plant function and health. As ecosystems warm and weather extremes intensify, these temperature data are increasingly vital for tracking vegetation changes and resilience.

Our Team

The TIR-ific Network is powered by a collaborative team of ecologists, engineers, and data scientists who are passionate about improving how we observe and understand the thermal behavior of ecosystems. We emphasize open science, field-tested methods, and community engagement.
